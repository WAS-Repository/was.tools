Project Founder

The World Archive System (WAS) is a project built to preserve humanity's collective knowledge using cutting-edge decentralized technology.

As the architect, I'm creating a robust platform using IPFS clusters, AI-powered search (leveraging high-performance GPU compute), and graph databases that connect information across domains.

The system is designed with a micro-services architecture spanning multiple specialized domains — from core storage (was.foundation), to developer tools (was.tools), to operational infrastructure (was.network), to public access endpoints (was.ngo).

I am currently a student at Hampton University's Scripps Howard School of Journalism and Communications, bringing a storyteller's perspective to this technical endeavor.

At WAS, we believe that information is our most precious resource. By preserving and connecting knowledge from our past, we create the foundation for a brighter future. Each artifact, each story, each datum we save becomes a building block for generations to come.

WAS—named for the was-scepter, sacred instrument of Thoth—continues to guide humanity toward knowledge and truth, connecting our ancestral wisdom with tomorrow's innovations.
